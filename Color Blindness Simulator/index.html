Understanding Color Vision Deficiencies

Color blindness affects approximately 1 in 12 men and 1 in 200 women globally. Designing with these conditions in mind ensures your products are accessible to everyone.

Protanopia

Red-blindness caused by the complete absence of red retinal photoreceptors (L-cones). Red colors appear dark, and individuals struggle to distinguish between red/green and orange/yellow-green hues.

Deuteranopia

Green-blindness caused by the complete absence of green retinal photoreceptors (M-cones). Green/red confusion is common, and it is the most frequent form of color vision deficiency.

Tritanopia

Blue-blindness caused by the complete absence of blue retinal photoreceptors (S-cones). This rare form makes it difficult to distinguish between blue/green and yellow/violet.

Achromatopsia

Complete color blindness (monochromacy) where the person sees only black, white, and shades of gray. Usually accompanied by sensitivity to bright light and visual acuity limitations.
